MUSICZONE

A music score tablet app for classical musicians

Overview

MUSICZONE is a sophisticated app designed for professional classical musicians, providing a seamless way to manage and access all their sheet music on a single device. The app features an expansive digital library where users can search for, download, and organize their musical scores with ease. In addition, the app includes a scanning tool that allows musicians to digitize physical sheet music, creating a personal collection within the app or uploading it directly to the cloud-based library for future use. MUSICZONE supports features like customizable annotations, real-time collaboration with other musicians, and advanced navigation tools for efficient sheet music reading, making it the ultimate solution for musicians who need quick, reliable access to their entire repertoire.

The Problem

Carrying around physical music sheets poses several long-term challenges for professional musicians:

  1. Physical strain from carrying heavy, bulky scores.
  2. Space limitations due to cluttered storage and difficulty organizing.
  3. Damage and wear to scores, especially rare or valuable editions.
  4. Inefficiency in finding specific music, wasting time.
  5. Difficulty managing updates or revisions to scores.
  6. Environmental impact from paper waste.

These challenges can affect a musician's health, productivity, and organization, making a digital solution like MUSICZONE a more efficient and sustainable option.

The Solution

MUSICZONE solves several problems faced by professional classical musicians:

  1. Eliminates the need to carry bulky physical scores by storing all music digitally.
  2. Saves space by organizing large collections in one app. Speeds up score retrieval with advanced search and categorization features.
  3. Provides access to rare or out-of-print music by allowing users to scan and digitize their own scores.
  4. Simplifies annotation by enabling digital marking and editing of scores.
  5. Facilitates collaboration with real-time score sharing and updates among musicians.
  6. Allows easy performance adaptation by quickly incorporating changes or revisions to scores.
  7. Reduces environmental impact by cutting down on paper usage.

In essence, MUSICZONE enhances organization, accessibility, collaboration, and sustainability for musicians.

Process

  1. Problem space research - researching the problem, analyzing data, and using AI tools in the process.
  2. Competition space analysis - analyzing the competition, identifying similar products, narrowing the space to the most popular ones, checking their strong and weak points.
  3. Ideation - identify target market, and user persona.
  4. Sitemap - a general idea of app's structure
  5. User Flows - general user scenarios and user stories
  6. Mockups - paper and pen stage - and Prototyping
  7. Design - in Figma
  8. Accessibility Testing - using Figma plugins and following WCAG guidelines
  9. Iterate the last 4 previous steps until the design is complete

Research

Target User Group Questionnaire

I created a list of questions that aims to uncover the needs, preferences, and pain points of classical musicians, helping to create a more user-centered and effective music score app.

Some of these questions include:

  1. How often do you currently use digital sheet music in your practice or performances?
  2. Do you currently use any other apps or tools in conjunction with your music score app (e.g., metronomes, tuners, sheet music editors)?
  3. How do you typically navigate through your music during practice or performance (e.g., by page number, section, or zooming in/out)?
